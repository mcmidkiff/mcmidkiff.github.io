Artists Celebrate Mac Miller's Life at Tribute Concert

On Halloween night, multiple artists got together to pay tribute to musician and rapper Mac Miller, who was found dead in his home in San Fernando Valley at the age of 26. The tribute concert featured artists such as Chance the Rapper, Travis Scott, John Mayer, Anderson. Paak, Vince Staples and many more. Each artist took a moment to reflect on Mac Miller's impact and legacy. “Mac, thank you for so many different things. Thank you for the music you made; thank you for the music that you provided other people with, for the opportunities that you provided me with, but overall, for the friendships that you built through your music and through your artistry,” said Chance the Rapper, who not only worked with Mac, but saw him as a friend and a legend in the music industry.

Drake and Travis Scott Release Video for "Sicko Mode"

Travis Scott's hit song "Sicko Mode" featuring Drake blew up the charts after his release of his 2018 album, Astroworld. The video was teased for over a month and after a while of anticipation, has finally dropped. The video features Drake and Travis Scott rapping in a supernatural looking version of Scott's hometown of Houston Texas. The video also pays tribute to another Houston artist and a large influence on Travis Scott's music, DJ Screw by including the famous Screwed Up Records & Tapes Store and an animated spray painting of DJ Screw rapping on a passing train car. The video can be watched on Youtube at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6ONRf7h3Mdk.

Kanye and Cudi's Collab Group, Kids See Ghosts, Make Live Debut

Kanye West and Kid Cudi released their collaborative self titled album, Kids See Ghosts, on June 8th, 2018. The album featured other artists such as Pusha T, Ty Dolla $ign, a sample from 1940's soul musician Louis Prima, and even a guitar sample by Kurt Cobain. There was a lot of hype around the release of the album due to Kanye's bold political statements and the return of Kid Cudi. Many fans enjoyed the album with songs like "4th Dimension", "Reborn", and "Cudi Montage". On November 11th, they made their live debut as a collab at Tyler, the Creator's music festival, Camp Flog Gnaw, performing their entire album as well as a couple of their solo songs.
